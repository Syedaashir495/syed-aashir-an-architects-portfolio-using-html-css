    An arboretum is a place where a variety of trees and other woody plants are cultivated for scientific, educational, and aesthetic purposes. In order to revive birds and insects that are in danger of extinction in Pakistan. This exceptional arboretum is a haven dedicated to the preservation of Pakistan's endangered birds and insects, rooted in the rich tradition of Lahore's Chahar Bagh. Nestled within a landscape teeming with lush greenery, it stands as a beacon of hope for these vulnerable creatures, interweaving the essence of vernacular architecture and ecological stewardship. The journey through this arboretum begins in the tranquil embrace of a Medical Garden, where the healing power of nature is on full display. It then transports visitors through time with the grandeur of Mughal Gardens, a testament to the enduring legacy of history. Transitioning gracefully, the Edible Gardens celebrate the land's bounty, while the Bird Garden becomes a refuge for species teetering on the brink of extinction. As visitors meander through the Prairie and Woodland Garden, they encounter the heart of biodiversity conservation, where delicate ecosystems flourish. A sustainable ethos echoes in the Rain and Riparian Garden, where the precious waters of the Ravi River are harnessed to nurture life. Each garden flows harmoniously with the river's currents, embodying the concept of water reuse, ensuring a sustainable future. Beyond its role as a sanctuary, this arboretum serves as an educational beacon, enlightening all who visit about the vital significance of safeguarding these delicate ecosystems, and the indispensable role these creatures play in maintaining the ecological balance.     Indigenous Medicinal Garden: Lahore has a rich history of traditional herbal medicine. Establishing an indigenous medicinal garden within the arboretum can highlight native plants with medicinal properties and their cultural significance. This garden can serve as a resource for education and research on traditional healing practices. Here, you'll find plants that have been used for centuries for their healing properties. It's a fascinating place to learn about traditional remedies and natural medicine in which Ashwaganda, Sweetflag, Rosemerry, Lavender, TeaTree, Curry patta, Asparagus officinalis, and papaverhybridum are used. As you walk through the grand entrance, you'll feel like you've traveled back in time to the Mughal gardens. you'll see the signature Mughal garden. This part of the arboretum is all about beauty and luxury. Drawing inspiration from Lahore's Mughal heritage, consider creating a garden that replicates the style of Mughal-era gardens. Incorporate native plants like jasmine, roses, pomegranate, and citrus trees, along with geometric layouts, water features, and ornamental structures such as pavilions or gazebos. The garden is meticulously designed with perfectly shaped hedges, fragrant roses, and elegant fountains. It's like stepping into a romantic fairytale.

    Woodland garden, where Native ferns, and wildflowers thrive in this peaceful retreat. Take a moment to enjoy the fresh smells and listen to the rustling leaves. Prairie garden, where wild native grasses, wildflowers, and forbs that are typical of prairie ecosystems. It can offer a diverse range of colors and textures while attracting butterflies, bees, and birds. Species include, Lavender, showy milkweed, Indian Grass, Opium poppy, Switchgrass, Western sunflowers Riparian garden where the arboretum meets the sparkling waterfront. It's a place where native aquatic plants and wetland flora coexist peacefully. The scene is calm and full of life. It's a beautiful reminder of how we can live in harmony with our natural surroundings. Species used in this garden are Indian Willow, Himalayan Alder, Neem (Azadirachta indica), Wild Olive, Indian Rose Chestnut. Rain garden this special part of the arboretum helps manage rainwater and promotes sustainable drainage. Native plants with amazing water-holding abilities grow here, creating a living tapestry that shows nature's adaptability and strength. Species include, Swamp Milkweed, Blue Flag Iris, Marsh Marigold, Swamp Sunflower, Blue Vervain, Golden Ragwort.

    Edible garden, which is bursting with delicious fruits, vegetables, and herbs. It's a place where you can learn about growing your own food and the importance of sustainable farming. You might even get to taste some freshly fruits like, Orange, Mango, berry, Guava, Almond, Garlic, lemon and Lychee. Bird garden, a haven for all kinds of birds. From small singing birds to majestic birds of prey, this garden is a paradise for bird lovers. It's a reminder of how important it is to protect and preserve our diverse feathered friends. Species include Gulmohar, Sumbal, Kachnar, Amaltas, Siris and Kikar ,Sunflower, Lavender, Ficus and Neem.
